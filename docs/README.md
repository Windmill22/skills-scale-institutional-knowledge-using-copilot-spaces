# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

## Overview

OctoAcme uses a structured project management approach covering initiation, planning, execution, risk management, release, and continuous improvement. These processes help us deliver high-quality projects on time, keep stakeholders aligned, and foster a culture of continuous learning.

Key principles:
- **Customer-first** — prioritize customer value and usability.
- **Iterative delivery** — deliver small, testable increments.
- **Clear ownership** — each project has a named Project Manager (PM) and Product Lead.
- **Data-informed decisions** — measure impact and iterate based on evidence.
- **Psychological safety** — encourage feedback and learning.

## Process Summary

### Initiation
Validate and authorize work before committing resources. Produce a Project One-pager with a problem statement, SMART goal, success metrics, stakeholder list, high-level timeline, and initial risk list. A decision gate confirms alignment before moving to planning.

### Planning
Turn an approved initiative into an actionable backlog. Activities include a kickoff meeting, backlog prioritization with acceptance criteria, effort estimation, Definition of Done, dependency mapping, and a release milestone plan.

### Execution & Tracking
Manage day-to-day delivery using standups, weekly syncs, and sprint demos. Teams follow a pull-request workflow (small PRs, CI gates, at least one approval), track quality through automated and manual testing, and escalate blockers through defined escalation paths.

### Risk Management & Communication
Maintain a Risk Register (ID, description, impact, likelihood, owner, mitigation, status) updated at every weekly sync. Stakeholder communication follows regular update cadences; incidents use a triage summary template and are followed by a blameless retrospective.

### Release & Deployment
Standardize releases (patch, minor, major) through pre-release gates (CI passing, acceptance criteria met, release notes drafted, rollback plan documented) and a deployment checklist covering staging smoke tests, production deployment, post-deploy verification, and stakeholder announcement.

### Retrospective & Continuous Improvement
After each sprint, release, or incident, run a timeboxed retrospective covering what went well, what could be improved, and 2–3 prioritized action items. Track actions in the project backlog and review them in weekly PM syncs.

### Roles & Personas
- **Project Manager (PM)** — coordinates delivery, schedules, risk, and communications.
- **Product Manager (PdM)** — defines outcomes, prioritizes the backlog, and measures success.
- **Developers** — implement features, collaborate on design and testability.
- **QA/Testing** — validate quality and acceptance criteria.
- **Stakeholders** — provide inputs and approvals.
